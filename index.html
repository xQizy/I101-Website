Background History

Kathleen Johnson, a pioneering mathematician, made significant contributions to space exploration. Born on August 26, 1918, she played a crucial role at NASA, breaking barriers and advancing our understanding of complex mathematical concepts.

Throughout her work, particularly during her work for NASA, Kathleen showcased her exceptional skills in mathematical analysis, making her an inspiration for future generations. Despite the fact that she lived during a time period where individuals of her specific gender and phenotype, she was able to become one of the most renowned aeronautical mathematicians of the space age.
